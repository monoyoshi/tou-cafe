red velvet cookies

  • (un?)salted butter
  • (granulated) sugar
  • (dark) brown sugar
  • large eggs
  • vanilla extract
  • vinegar
  • red (or blue?) food coloring/gel
  • (all-purpose) flour
  • cocoa powder
  • cornstarch
  • baking soda
  • salt
  • white chocolate chips
  • stand mixer with paddle attachment
  • cookie scoop
  • 2 baking sheets
  • parchment paper
  • refrigerator
  • oven at 350°f
  • big cool-looking plate

to be honest, I mashed 3 different red velvet cookie recipes together on accident. it's pretty good regardless :)

soften 1 cup butter
  • butter can be unsalted or salted
  • to soften, let it sit at room temperature for ~30 minutes until you can make a dent by lightly poking
beat the butter, 1 cup (granulated) sugar, and 2/3 cup (dark) brown sugar using the stand mixer with the paddle attachment
beat in 2 large eggs, ~6 tsp vanilla extract, 2 tsp vinegar, and 2 tsp red food coloring/gel
  • I suppose any color could work, but I've never tried adding blue food coloring (people don't want me to)
beat in 3 1/3 cup (all-purpose) flour, 3 tbsp cocoa powder, 4 tsp cornstarch, 1 1/2 tsp baking soda, and 1/2 tsp salt
beat in 1 cup white chocolate chips until thick and sticky
refrigerate cookie dough up to overnight
preheat the oven to 350°f
line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper
form the cookie dough into loosely packed balls about 1 1/4 tbsp in size and place 2 inches apart on the baking sheets
  • use a cookie scoop to help
  • at the very least, be able to place cookies in a 6x3 grid
bake for 9 minutes, then for ~3 minutes or until the tops are just set
let them chill at room temperature for 5+ minutes
transfer to a cool-looking plate big enough to stack the cookies on nicely
return home
